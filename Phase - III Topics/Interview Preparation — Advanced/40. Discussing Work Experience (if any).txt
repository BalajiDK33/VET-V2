Concept: Interview Preparation — Discussing Work Experience
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do
Note: Use the exact roleplay questions below in the conversation roleplay section.

Sentence Structure: "In my previous role, I was responsible for [task], which helped me develop [skill]."

Roleplay Questions:

1. The interviewer asks about your previous work experience. Share your role and the skill you developed.
2. The interviewer asks what tasks you handled in your last job. Share your responsibility and what you gained.
3. The interviewer asks how your past experience is relevant to this role. Share your task and the skill it gave you.
4. The interviewer asks what you learned from your internship. Share your responsibility and the skill developed.
5. The interviewer asks how your work experience helped you grow. Share your role and key takeaway.

Examples:

"In my previous role, I was responsible for data entry, which helped me develop attention to detail."
"In my previous role, I was responsible for customer support, which helped me develop communication skills."
"In my previous role, I was responsible for team coordination, which helped me develop leadership skills."
"In my previous role, I was responsible for social media management, which helped me develop creativity."
