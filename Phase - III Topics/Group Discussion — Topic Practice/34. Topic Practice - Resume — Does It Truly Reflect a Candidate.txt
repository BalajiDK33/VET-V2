Concept: Group Discussion — Resume — Does It Truly Reflect a Candidate
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do
Note: Use the exact roleplay questions below in the conversation roleplay section.

GD topics to use for sentences: Overqualification — Boon or Bane, Hard Skills vs. Soft Skills — Which Matters More, Lateral Hiring vs. Fresher Hiring, Resume — Does It Truly Reflect a Candidate, Leadership — Born or Made, Age Limit in Recruitment — Fair or Unfair, Work Pressure vs. Work-Life Balance, Contract Jobs vs. Permanent Jobs

Sentence Structure 1: "[Topic] offers [advantage]."
Sentence Structure 2: "While [topic] offers [advantage], it also has a [drawback]."

Roleplay Questions:

1. Priya says a resume is the best way to judge a candidate. Share your view using Structure 1.
2. Rahul says resumes do not show the real personality of a candidate. Respond using Structure 2.
3. Ram says a well-written resume always gets shortlisted. Share your view using Structure 1.
4. Sneha says resumes can be misleading and should not be trusted fully. Respond using Structure 2.
5. Arjun says practical tests are better than resumes for judging candidates. Share your view using Structure 2.

Examples:

"A resume offers a quick overview of the candidate's profile."
"A resume offers clarity and structure to the recruitment process."
"While a resume offers basic screening, it also has a risk of missing true talent."
"While a resume offers organised information, it also has the challenge of being inaccurate."
