Concept: Group Discussion — Work Pressure vs. Work-Life Balance
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do
Note: Use the exact roleplay questions below in the conversation roleplay section.

GD topics to use for sentences: Overqualification — Boon or Bane, Hard Skills vs. Soft Skills — Which Matters More, Lateral Hiring vs. Fresher Hiring, Resume — Does It Truly Reflect a Candidate, Leadership — Born or Made, Age Limit in Recruitment — Fair or Unfair, Work Pressure vs. Work-Life Balance, Contract Jobs vs. Permanent Jobs

Sentence Structure 1: "[Topic] offers [advantage]."
Sentence Structure 2: "While [topic] offers [advantage], it also has a [drawback]."

Roleplay Questions:

1. What does work pressure offer to an employee? Use Structure 1.
2. While work pressure offers productivity, what risk does it also bring? Respond using Structure 2.
3. What does work-life balance offer to an employee? Use Structure 1.
4. While work-life balance offers better health, what challenge does it also bring? Give your answer using Structure 2.
5. What does work pressure offer to career growth? Use Structure 1.

Examples:

"Work pressure offers higher motivation and better productivity."
"Work-life balance offers better health and personal well-being."
"While work pressure offers faster results, it also has a risk of stress and burnout."
"While work-life balance offers well-being, it also has a risk of slower career growth."
