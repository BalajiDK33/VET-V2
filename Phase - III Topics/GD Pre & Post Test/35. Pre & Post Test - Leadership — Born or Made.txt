Concept: Group Discussion — Leadership — Born or Made
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do

Sentence Structure 1: "[Topic] offers [advantage]."
Sentence Structure 2: "While [topic] offers [advantage], it also has a [drawback]."

Pre Test Questions:

1. "Born leadership offers natural confidence and strong decision-making ability."
2. "While born leadership offers natural talent, it also has a risk of being inflexible."
3. "Born leadership offers an instinctive ability to guide and motivate others."
4. "While born leadership offers confidence, it also has a challenge of not listening to others."
5. "Born leadership offers strong communication and vision from an early age."
6. "While born leadership offers strong instincts, it also has a risk of overlooking team input."
7. "Born leadership offers a natural sense of responsibility and initiative."
8. "While born leadership offers quick decisions, it also has a drawback of being overconfident."
9. "Born leadership offers quick thinking and the ability to handle pressure."
10. "While born leadership offers natural ability, it also has a risk of lacking structured thinking."

Post Test Questions:

1. "Learned leadership offers adaptability and growth through experience."
2. "While learned leadership offers skill development, it also has the need for constant training."
3. "Learned leadership offers practical skills gained through training and practice."
4. "While learned leadership offers adaptability, it also has a risk of lacking natural confidence."
5. "Learned leadership offers the ability to develop leadership at any stage of life."
6. "While learned leadership offers structured growth, it also has a challenge of being time-consuming."
7. "Learned leadership offers a structured approach to managing people and tasks."
8. "While learned leadership offers flexibility, it also has a risk of being less instinctive."
9. "Learned leadership offers continuous improvement through feedback and learning."
10. "While learned leadership offers practical skills, it also has a drawback of depending on external guidance."
