Concept: Group Discussion — Lateral Hiring vs. Fresher Hiring
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do

Sentence Structure 1: "[Topic] offers [advantage]."
Sentence Structure 2: "While [topic] offers [advantage], it also has a [drawback]."

Pre Test Questions:

1. "Lateral hiring offers immediate expertise to the organization."
2. "While lateral hiring offers experience, it also has a higher cost of recruitment."
3. "Lateral hiring offers experienced professionals who need less training."
4. "While lateral hiring offers expertise, it also has a risk of the candidate not fitting the culture."
5. "Lateral hiring offers industry knowledge that benefits the team quickly."
6. "While lateral hiring offers faster results, it also has a challenge of higher salary expectations."
7. "Lateral hiring offers faster productivity from day one."
8. "While lateral hiring offers industry knowledge, it also has a risk of limited fresh ideas."
9. "Lateral hiring offers specialized skills for senior and technical roles."
10. "While lateral hiring offers immediate contribution, it also has a drawback of less flexibility."

Post Test Questions:

1. "Fresher hiring offers new energy and fresh perspectives to the team."
2. "While fresher hiring offers adaptability, it also has a longer training period."
3. "Fresher hiring offers candidates who are easy to train and mould."
4. "While fresher hiring offers enthusiasm, it also has a risk of lack of experience."
5. "Fresher hiring offers long-term loyalty and commitment to the company."
6. "While fresher hiring offers cost savings, it also has a challenge of slower productivity initially."
7. "Fresher hiring offers cost-effective recruitment for entry-level roles."
8. "While fresher hiring offers loyalty, it also has a risk of the candidate leaving after gaining experience."
9. "Fresher hiring offers innovative thinking and enthusiasm to the workplace."
10. "While fresher hiring offers fresh ideas, it also has a drawback of needing more supervision."
