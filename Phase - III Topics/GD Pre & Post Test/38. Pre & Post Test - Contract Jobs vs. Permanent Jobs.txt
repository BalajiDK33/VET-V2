Concept: Group Discussion — Contract Jobs vs. Permanent Jobs
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do

Sentence Structure 1: "[Topic] offers [advantage]."
Sentence Structure 2: "While [topic] offers [advantage], it also has a [drawback]."

Pre Test Questions:

1. "Contract jobs offer flexibility and diverse work experience."
2. "While contract jobs offer flexibility, it also has a lack of job security."
3. "Contract jobs offer the freedom to choose projects and work hours."
4. "While contract jobs offer variety, it also has a risk of inconsistent income."
5. "Contract jobs offer exposure to different industries and companies."
6. "While contract jobs offer higher pay, it also has a challenge of no employee benefits."
7. "Contract jobs offer higher pay rates for specialized short-term work."
8. "While contract jobs offer independence, it also has a risk of isolation from the team."
9. "Contract jobs offer the ability to build a wide professional network."
10. "While contract jobs offer exposure, it also has a drawback of no long-term career growth."

Post Test Questions:

1. "Permanent jobs offer stability and long-term career growth."
2. "While permanent jobs offer stability, it also has limited variety in work."
3. "Permanent jobs offer benefits such as health insurance and paid leave."
4. "While permanent jobs offer benefits, it also has a risk of becoming monotonous over time."
5. "Permanent jobs offer a clear career path and promotion opportunities."
6. "While permanent jobs offer career growth, it also has a challenge of less flexibility."
7. "Permanent jobs offer a sense of security and financial stability."
8. "While permanent jobs offer security, it also has a risk of limited earning potential."
9. "Permanent jobs offer stronger team bonds and a sense of belonging."
10. "While permanent jobs offer belonging, it also has a drawback of slower salary increases."
