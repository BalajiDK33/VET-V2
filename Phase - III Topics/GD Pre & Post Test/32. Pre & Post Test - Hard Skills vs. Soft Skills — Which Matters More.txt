Concept: Group Discussion — Hard Skills vs. Soft Skills — Which Matters More
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do

Sentence Structure 1: "[Topic] offers [advantage]."
Sentence Structure 2: "While [topic] offers [advantage], it also has a [drawback]."

Pre Test Questions:

1. "Hard skills offer technical ability for better job performance."
2. "While hard skills offer technical expertise, it also has a limitation of poor interpersonal skills."
3. "Hard skills offer the knowledge needed to complete specific tasks."
4. "While hard skills offer job-specific knowledge, it also has a risk of being outdated quickly."
5. "Hard skills offer measurable proof of a candidate's capabilities."
6. "While hard skills offer precision in tasks, it also has a drawback of limited creativity."
7. "Hard skills offer a strong foundation for technical roles."
8. "While hard skills offer strong technical output, it also has a challenge of poor communication."
9. "Hard skills offer confidence in handling job-specific tools and software."
10. "While hard skills offer measurable results, it also has a risk of ignoring emotional intelligence."

Post Test Questions:

1. "Soft skills offer better teamwork and communication in the workplace."
2. "While soft skills offer better communication, it also has a lack of technical knowledge."
3. "Soft skills offer the ability to build strong professional relationships."
4. "While soft skills offer teamwork ability, it also has a risk of lacking job-specific expertise."
5. "Soft skills offer better adaptability in changing work environments."
6. "While soft skills offer flexibility, it also has the challenge of being difficult to measure."
7. "Soft skills offer leadership and problem-solving abilities."
8. "While soft skills offer better relationships, it also has a risk of poor task execution."
9. "Soft skills offer improved collaboration across teams and departments."
10. "While soft skills offer adaptability, it also has a drawback of being insufficient for technical roles."
