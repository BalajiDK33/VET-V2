Concept: Group Discussion — Work Pressure vs. Work-Life Balance
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do

Sentence Structure 1: "[Topic] offers [advantage]."
Sentence Structure 2: "While [topic] offers [advantage], it also has a [drawback]."

Pre Test Questions:

1. "Work pressure offers higher motivation and better productivity."
2. "While work pressure offers faster results, it also has a risk of stress and burnout."
3. "Work pressure offers a sense of urgency that drives faster results."
4. "While work pressure offers motivation, it also has a challenge of affecting mental health."
5. "Work pressure offers the opportunity to develop resilience and discipline."
6. "While work pressure offers productivity, it also has a risk of reducing job satisfaction."
7. "Work pressure offers faster career growth through challenging tasks."
8. "While work pressure offers better performance, it also has a drawback of poor work quality over time."
9. "Work pressure offers a competitive environment that pushes employees to perform better."
10. "While work pressure offers career growth, it also has a risk of increasing employee turnover."

Post Test Questions:

1. "Work-life balance offers better health and personal well-being."
2. "While work-life balance offers well-being, it also has a risk of slower career growth."
3. "Work-life balance offers more time for family and personal development."
4. "While work-life balance offers happiness, it also has a challenge of reduced output during peak times."
5. "Work-life balance offers improved mental health and reduced stress levels."
6. "While work-life balance offers rest, it also has a risk of missing important deadlines."
7. "Work-life balance offers higher job satisfaction and employee loyalty."
8. "While work-life balance offers satisfaction, it also has a drawback of being misunderstood as low ambition."
9. "Work-life balance offers better focus and concentration during working hours."
10. "While work-life balance offers personal time, it also has a challenge of managing client expectations."
