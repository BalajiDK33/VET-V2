Concept: Group Discussion — Resume — Does It Truly Reflect a Candidate
Level: A1
Lesson Type: Can Do

Sentence Structure 1: "[Topic] offers [advantage]."
Sentence Structure 2: "While [topic] offers [advantage], it also has a [drawback]."

Pre Test Questions:

1. "A resume offers a quick overview of the candidate's profile."
2. "While a resume offers basic screening, it also has a risk of missing true talent."
3. "A resume offers clarity and structure to the recruitment process."
4. "While a resume offers organised information, it also has the challenge of being inaccurate."
5. "A resume offers a summary of the candidate's education and experience."
6. "While a resume offers a professional overview, it also has a risk of not showing real personality."
7. "A resume offers recruiters a first impression of the applicant."
8. "While a resume offers easy comparison, it also has a drawback of being easily exaggerated."
9. "A resume offers an easy way to compare multiple candidates."
10. "While a resume offers structured details, it also has a risk of hiding weaknesses."

Post Test Questions:

1. "A resume offers documented proof of achievements and qualifications."
2. "While a resume offers a first impression, it also has a challenge of not showing soft skills."
3. "A resume offers a professional format to present work history."
4. "While a resume offers recruitment efficiency, it also has a risk of judging candidates unfairly."
5. "A resume offers a time-saving tool for initial candidate screening."
6. "While a resume offers documented skills, it also has a drawback of not reflecting attitude."
7. "A resume offers a highlight of the candidate's key skills and strengths."
8. "While a resume offers shortlisting ease, it also has a risk of rejecting deserving candidates."
9. "A resume offers a structured way to shortlist candidates for interviews."
10. "While a resume offers professional history, it also has a challenge of being too formal and limiting."
